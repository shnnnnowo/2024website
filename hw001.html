Taipei, Oct. 4 (CNA) Taiwan's Central Weather Administration (CWA) has lifted its sea and land typhoon warnings after Tropical Storm Krathon was downgraded to a tropical depression. The weather agency canceled the typhoon warning at 5:30 a.m. Friday, saying that the storm had been downgraded to a tropical depression 30 minutes earlier, with its center nearly stationary.

At 5 a.m., the center of Krathon was located 20 kilometers northeast of Kaohsiung, moving slowly southward. It was carrying maximum sustained winds of 54 kilometers per hour, with gusts of up to 72 kph, according to the CWA.
